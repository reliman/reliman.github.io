Privacy in Action (Clue Room Style Training, Gamification)

About Project

I wanted to make sure that the information that was taught to users during mandatory IT training were applied to their physical office settings.Users were told that they were going to attend an informal training on IT. However, when they entered the conference room they were in an “escape room” style office with privacy and security issues on this pseudo person’s physical work space.

Research Question

How can I make sure that privacy and security training topics are applied outside of the online training setting?

Methods

[Quantitative] Gamified Survey & [Qualitative] Unstructured Interviews 

Gamified Survey: This training/research study involved a gamified survey where users had to work together to answer each question that I asked them. The questions were based on different areas where I had set up a security or privacy problem that they had to solve. For example, let’s say I had a fake computer and the user had left their computer logged in, I would ask the participants why it was not good security practice to keep their computer logged in (i.e. how is this employee not abiding by safe security practices with their computer? Answer: because employees should never leave their computer unlocked while not actively using their computer). I was able to collect information from each user’s answers to see what areas were growth areas for security and privacy education.

Unstructured Interviews: I took note of the feedback that participants gave when they explained problems/questions that were easy and those that were not as easy. These informal interviews allowed me to gauge the perceptions from participants (for example: “wow, I didn’t think about such-and-such issue as being a privacy issue until I saw an example of it on a fake employee’s desk”).
